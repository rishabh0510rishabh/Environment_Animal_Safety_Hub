# Trophic Downgrading After Predator Removal - Feature Documentation

## Issue #1531: Trophic Downgrading After Predator Removal

### Overview
This educational feature examines the ecosystem-level consequences of apex predator loss, exploring how the removal of top predators leads to trophic downgrading, herbivore overpopulation, and subsequent vegetation collapse. The page provides comprehensive information about trophic cascades, predator-prey dynamics, and the cascading effects throughout food webs.

### Features Implemented

#### 1. **Educational Content Structure**
- Hero section introducing trophic downgrading crisis
- Overview of apex predator roles in ecosystem regulation
- Detailed mechanisms of trophic cascades
- Case studies of predator removal consequences

#### 2. **Trophic Cascade Mechanisms**
- Direct predator-prey relationships
- Indirect effects on herbivore populations
- Vegetation changes and habitat alteration
- Biodiversity loss and ecosystem simplification

#### 3. **Apex Predator Functions**
- Population control of herbivores
- Behavioral changes in prey species
- Landscape of fear and habitat use
- Keystone species effects

#### 4. **Herbivore Overpopulation Impacts**
- Vegetation depletion and desertification
- Soil erosion and land degradation
- Competition with native species
- Economic impacts on agriculture and forestry

#### 5. **Vegetation Collapse Consequences**
- Loss of plant biodiversity
- Changes in water cycles and soil retention
- Increased fire risk and altered succession
- Long-term ecosystem recovery challenges

#### 6. **Case Studies**
- Wolf reintroduction in Yellowstone
- Sea otter decline and kelp forest collapse
- African lion population declines
- Shark removal in coastal ecosystems

#### 7. **Solutions and Restoration**
- Predator reintroduction programs
- Protected area management
- Sustainable hunting regulations
- Habitat connectivity restoration

#### 8. **Interactive Elements**
- Animated food web diagrams
- Predator removal simulation tool
- Before/after ecosystem comparison
- Trophic level impact calculator
