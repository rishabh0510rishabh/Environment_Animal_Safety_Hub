Reporting Guidelines

When to Report

  • Animals showing signs of abuse, neglect, or injury
  • Animals left outside in extreme weather without shelter
  • Animals that appear malnourished or dehydrated
  • Illegal animal fighting or breeding operations
  • Wildlife in distress or dangerous situations
  • Abandoned animals or hoarding situations

When NOT to Report

  • Healthy stray animals (contact local shelters instead)
  • Wildlife in their natural habitat
  • Domestic disputes without animal involvement
  • General complaints about pet ownership
  • Situations that don't involve animal welfare

Your Safety First

  • Never confront suspected abusers directly
  • Report from a safe location away from the incident
  • Do not attempt to rescue animals yourself
  • Take photos from a safe distance if possible
  • Keep your identity anonymous if you feel threatened

Frequently Asked Questions

What happens after I submit a report?

Your report is reviewed by trained professionals who will assess the situation and determine the appropriate response. High-priority cases receive immediate attention, while others are investigated within 24-48 hours.

Is my report anonymous?

Yes, you can choose to remain anonymous. However, providing contact information allows us to follow up with you for additional details if needed.

How long does it take to respond?

Emergency situations are addressed immediately. Other cases are typically investigated within 24-72 hours, depending on the severity and available resources.

Can I follow up on my report?

If you provided contact information, we can update you on the status. You can also call the hotline to check on the progress of your report.

What if I'm wrong about the situation?

All reports are investigated thoroughly and professionally. If the situation doesn't warrant intervention, no action will be taken, and your report will be closed.
