Common Pet Diseases

Learn to recognize symptoms and understand treatment options for common pet illnesses.

Common Canine Diseases

Understanding the most prevalent health issues affecting dogs and how to prevent them.

Parvovirus

Highly contagious viral disease affecting gastrointestinal tract. Spread through contact with infected feces. Symptoms: vomiting, diarrhea (often bloody), lethargy, loss of appetite. Prevention: Vaccination is crucial.

Distemper

Serious viral illness affecting respiratory, gastrointestinal, and nervous systems. Spread through airborne exposure. Symptoms: fever, nasal discharge, coughing, seizures. Prevention: Core vaccination required.

Heartworm Disease

Parasitic worms transmitted by mosquitoes that live in heart and lungs. Symptoms: cough, fatigue, weight loss, difficulty breathing. Prevention: Monthly preventive medication year-round.

Arthritis

Joint inflammation common in older dogs and certain breeds. Symptoms: stiffness, limping, difficulty rising, reduced activity. Prevention: Weight management, joint supplements, regular exercise.

Common Feline Diseases

Health issues frequently seen in cats and preventive measures for feline wellness.

Feline Leukemia (FeLV)

Viral infection that weakens immune system and can lead to cancer. Spread through saliva, nasal secretions, urine. Symptoms: weight loss, poor coat, infections. Prevention: Vaccination and keeping cats indoors.

Feline Immunodeficiency (FIV)

Viral infection similar to human HIV, affecting immune system. Spread primarily through bite wounds. Symptoms: recurrent infections, weight loss, dental disease. Prevention: Keep cats indoors, avoid fights.

Upper Respiratory Infections

Common viral/bacterial infections similar to human colds. Symptoms: sneezing, nasal discharge, eye discharge, fever. Prevention: Vaccination, reduce stress, good nutrition.

Chronic Kidney Disease

Progressive kidney dysfunction common in older cats. Symptoms: increased thirst, weight loss, poor appetite, vomiting. Prevention: Regular checkups, proper hydration, kidney-friendly diet.

Small Pet Health Issues

Common health problems in rabbits, guinea pigs, hamsters, and other small pets.

Dental Problems

Overgrown teeth in rabbits and rodents. Teeth grow continuously. Symptoms: drooling, difficulty eating, weight loss. Prevention: Proper diet with hay, regular dental checks.

Respiratory Infections

Common in small pets due to stress and poor conditions. Symptoms: sneezing, nasal discharge, lethargy, breathing difficulties. Prevention: Clean housing, proper ventilation, reduce stress.

Gastrointestinal Stasis

Serious condition in rabbits where digestive system slows or stops. Symptoms: reduced appetite, small or no fecal pellets, lethargy. Prevention: High-fiber diet, regular exercise, hydration.

Obesity

Common issue in many small pets due to improper diet and lack of exercise. Leads to other health problems. Prevention: Proper portion control, appropriate diet, regular exercise.

Avian Health Concerns

Common diseases affecting pet birds and preventive care strategies.

Psittacosis

Bacterial infection (Chlamydia psittaci) that can affect humans (zoonotic). Symptoms: respiratory distress, eye/nasal discharge, lethargy. Prevention: Quarantine new birds, good hygiene, vet checks.

Nutritional Deficiencies

Common in birds fed seed-only diets. Symptoms: poor feather quality, beak abnormalities, weakness. Prevention: Balanced pelleted diet, fresh vegetables, calcium supplements.

Respiratory Issues

Sensitive respiratory systems easily affected by environmental factors. Symptoms: wheezing, tail bobbing, open-mouth breathing. Prevention: Avoid fumes, proper ventilation, clean environment.

Feather Plucking

Behavioral or medical issue where birds remove their own feathers. Causes: stress, boredom, skin conditions. Prevention: Environmental enrichment, proper diet, reduce stress.

Vaccination & Prevention

Essential vaccinations and preventive measures to protect your pets from common diseases.

Core Vaccinations

Essential vaccines recommended for all pets regardless of lifestyle

  • Dogs: Rabies, Distemper, Parvovirus, Adenovirus
  • Cats: Rabies, Feline Distemper (Panleukopenia)
  • Frequency: Initial series followed by boosters
  • Legal Requirement: Rabies vaccine required by law
  • Protection: Provides immunity against deadly diseases

Non-Core Vaccinations

Recommended based on pet's lifestyle, environment, and risk factors

  • Dogs: Lyme, Leptospirosis, Bordetella
  • Cats: Feline Leukemia, Feline Immunodeficiency
  • Considerations: Outdoor access, boarding, travel
  • Risk Assessment: Discuss with your veterinarian
  • Customized: Tailored to individual pet needs

Vaccination Schedule

Recommended timeline for pet vaccinations and preventive care

  • Puppies/Kittens: Start at 6-8 weeks, every 3-4 weeks until 16 weeks
  • Adult Pets: Annual wellness exams with vaccine review
  • Senior Pets: Biannual exams, adjusted vaccines as needed
  • Documentation: Keep accurate vaccination records
  • Reminders: Set up automatic reminders with your vet

Disease Prevention Strategies

Proactive measures to keep your pets healthy and prevent common illnesses.

Nutrition & Diet

Balanced Diet

Feed species-appropriate, age-specific, high-quality food with proper nutrients.

Weight Management

Maintain healthy weight through portion control and regular exercise to prevent obesity-related diseases.

Fresh Water

Provide clean, fresh water daily to support all bodily functions and prevent dehydration.

Environment & Hygiene

Clean Living Space

Regular cleaning of bedding, litter boxes, cages, and feeding areas to prevent disease spread.

Parasite Control

Regular flea, tick, and worm prevention medications as recommended by your veterinarian.

Stress Reduction

Create calm environments, provide enrichment, and minimize changes to reduce stress-related illnesses.

Veterinary Care

Regular Checkups

Annual wellness exams for adult pets, biannual for seniors, to catch issues early.

Dental Care

Regular dental cleanings and home dental care to prevent periodontal disease.

Early Intervention

Address health concerns promptly rather than waiting for symptoms to worsen.
