Learn how responsible pet ownership contributes to wildlife conservation. Protect native species while caring for your pets through sustainable practices.

How Pets Impact Wildlife

Understanding the relationship between domestic animals and native wildlife is crucial for conservation.

Predation Risks

Outdoor cats are significant predators of native wildlife

  • Cats kill 1.3-4 billion birds annually in US
  • 6-22 billion small mammals affected
  • Reptiles and amphibians also impacted
  • Native species most vulnerable

Disease Transmission

Pets can spread diseases to wildlife populations

  • Toxoplasmosis from cats to marine life
  • Canine distemper to wild carnivores
  • Parvovirus transmission risks
  • Rabies concerns in wildlife

Positive Contributions

Responsible pet owners can support conservation

  • Creating wildlife-friendly yards
  • Supporting conservation organizations
  • Educating others about coexistence
  • Adopting sustainable pet products

Solutions & Mitigation

Practical ways to reduce pet-wildlife conflicts

  • Keep cats indoors or supervised
  • Use wildlife-safe pet products
  • Proper waste management
  • Support habitat conservation

Conservation Guidelines for Pet Owners

Follow these guidelines to minimize your pet's impact on local wildlife.

Indoor & Outdoor Management

Keep Cats Indoors

Provide indoor enrichment and safe outdoor access via catios or leashed walks.

Supervise Outdoor Time

Always accompany pets outside and keep dogs on leashes in natural areas.

Secure Outdoor Areas

Use wildlife-proof fencing and enclosures to prevent escapes and wildlife entry.

Sustainable Pet Products

Eco-Friendly Food

Choose sustainably sourced pet food with minimal environmental impact.

Wildlife-Safe Toys

Avoid toys with real feathers or fur from wild animals.

Biodegradable Waste Bags

Use compostable bags for pet waste when proper disposal is available.

Habitat & Garden Care

Create Wildlife Corridors

Leave natural areas in your yard for wildlife movement and habitat.

Native Plant Gardens

Plant native species that support local wildlife while being pet-safe.

Safe Bird Feeding

Place bird feeders in areas inaccessible to pets and clean regularly.

Protecting Vulnerable Species

Learn which species are most affected by pet interactions and how to protect them.

Bird Conservation & Pets

Birds are particularly vulnerable to pet predation and disturbance. Learn how to create bird-safe environments.

Key Threats

Cat predation, window collisions from indoor cats, nest disturbance by dogs, competition for resources.

Protection Measures

Keep cats indoors during nesting season, use window decals, keep dogs leashed in nesting areas, provide native plants.

Species Most Affected

Ground-nesting birds, migratory songbirds, fledglings, and endangered species like the Florida Scrub-Jay.

Reptile Conservation & Pets

Reptiles face unique threats from pet interactions, especially in urban-wildland interface areas.

Key Threats

Predation by cats and dogs, habitat destruction from digging, vehicle collisions when chased by pets.

Protection Measures

Supervise pets in reptile habitats, create rock piles and logs as refuge, avoid pesticide use in yards.

Species Most Affected

Turtles, lizards, snakes (especially during hibernation), and endangered species like Gopher Tortoises.

Small Mammal Conservation

Small mammals are frequent targets of pet predation and face habitat competition.

Key Threats

Direct predation, nest destruction, disease transmission, competition for den sites.

Protection Measures

Keep pets indoors at dawn/dusk, provide escape routes in fencing, maintain natural brush piles.

Species Most Affected

Rabbits, squirrels, chipmunks, mice, voles, and endangered species like the Key Largo Woodrat.

Amphibian Conservation

Amphibians are sensitive to pet disturbances and environmental changes caused by pets.

Key Threats

Water contamination from pet waste, predation of tadpoles and adults, habitat trampling.

Protection Measures

Keep pets away from ponds and wetlands, use pet-safe pest control, maintain buffer zones.

Species Most Affected

Frogs, toads, salamanders, newts, and endangered species like the California Red-legged Frog.
