Animal Psychology

Understanding Animal Behaviour

Learn to interpret animal body language, vocalizations, and behaviors. Understand what your pet is trying to communicate for better care, stronger bonds, and improved animal welfare.

93% Of communication is non-verbal
50+ Distinct vocalizations in dogs
16 Facial expressions in cats

Animal Communication Methods

Animals communicate through various signals. Learn to recognize and interpret these messages.

Visual Signals

Body language, facial expressions, and posture that convey emotions and intentions.

  • Ear position: Forward (interested), back (nervous), flat (fearful)
  • Tail carriage: High (confident), low (submissive), tucked (fearful)
  • Body posture: Arched back (defensive), lowered (submissive)
  • Eye contact: Direct (challenge), avoiding (submission)
  • Hair/fur: Raised hackles (arousal, fear, or aggression)

Vocalizations

Sounds animals make to communicate different needs, emotions, and warnings.

  • Barks: Alert, play, fear, demand, loneliness
  • Meows: Greeting, demand, complaint, distress
  • Purring: Contentment, self-soothing, pain relief
  • Growls: Warning, possession, fear, play (context matters)
  • Whines/whimpers: Anxiety, excitement, attention-seeking

Behavioral Signals

Actions and routines that communicate needs, emotions, and social status.

  • Play bows: "Let's play" invitation (front down, rear up)
  • Rolling over: Submission or request for belly rubs
  • Kneading: Contentment, nesting behavior, scent marking
  • Zoomies: Frenetic random activity periods (FRAPs)
  • Resource guarding: Protecting valued items (food, toys)

Species-Specific Behaviors

Each species has unique behavioral patterns and communication methods.

Understanding Canine Behavior

Dogs are social animals with complex communication systems inherited from their wolf ancestors.

Happy/Content Signals

Relaxed body: Loose, wiggly movements, soft eyes

Play bow: Front down, rear up, wagging tail

Soft panting: With relaxed facial muscles

Wagging tail: Wide, sweeping motions at medium height

Anxious/Stressed Signals

Lip licking: When not around food (calming signal)

Yawning: When not tired (stress signal)

Pacing: Restless movement, inability to settle

Whale eye: Showing whites of eyes (anxiety)

Fearful/Aggressive Signals

Stiff body: Frozen posture, tense muscles

Growling with teeth: Clear warning to back off

Tail between legs: With ears back (fearful)

Direct staring: Hard eye contact (challenge)

Understanding Feline Behavior

Cats are solitary hunters with subtle communication systems often misunderstood by humans.

Content/Relaxed Signals

Slow blinking: "Cat kisses" showing trust

Upright tail: With slight curve at tip (greeting)

Kneading: "Making biscuits" on soft surfaces

Purring: With relaxed body posture

Anxious/Stressed Signals

Dilated pupils: In normal light (arousal/fear)

Twitching tail: Especially at the tip (irritation)

Ears sideways: "Airplane ears" (uneasy)

Hiding: Seeking enclosed spaces (stress)

Fearful/Aggressive Signals

Arched back: With piloerection (fear/defense)

Flattened ears: Pressed against head (fear)

Hissing/growling: Clear warning signals

Swatting/biting: After clear warning signals ignored

Small Pet Behavior

Rabbits, guinea pigs, hamsters, and other small pets have unique behavioral patterns.

Happy/Content Signals

Binkying (rabbits): Joyful leaps and twists

Popcorning (guinea pigs): Happy hops and twists

Purring (guinea pigs): Deep rumbling when content

Flattening (hamsters): Relaxed pancake pose

Anxious/Stressed Signals

Freezing: Motionless (fear response)

Teeth grinding (rabbits): Loud teeth grinding (pain)

Hiding constantly: Refusing to come out

Over-grooming: Creating bald patches

Fearful/Aggressive Signals

Thumping (rabbits): Warning of danger

Biting/barging: When threatened or cornered

Screaming (guinea pigs): Extreme fear/pain

Lunging: Defensive aggression

Avian Behavior

Birds are intelligent, social creatures with complex communication systems.

Happy/Content Signals

Singing/whistling: Content vocalizations

Preening: Grooming self or bonded human

Beak grinding: Contentment (especially before sleep)

Playful behavior: Tossing toys, hanging upside down

Anxious/Stressed Signals

Feather plucking: Self-destructive behavior

Pacing: Repetitive back-and-forth movement

Panting: When not hot (stress indicator)

Screaming: Prolonged loud vocalizations

Fearful/Aggressive Signals

Biting/lunging: With pinned eyes (aggression)

Hissing/growling: Some species make these sounds

Fluffed feathers: With crouching (fearful)

Eye pinning: Rapid pupil dilation/constriction

Understanding Animal Emotions

Animals experience a range of emotions. Learn to recognize and respond appropriately.

Positive Emotions

Contentment/Happiness

Relaxed body posture, soft eyes, normal breathing, engaging in voluntary activities like play or exploration.

Excitement/Anticipation

Eager body language, focused attention, possibly vocalizing, quick movements, wagging tail (dogs).

Affection/Bonding

Seeking proximity, gentle physical contact, grooming behaviors, sharing resources, following.

Negative Emotions

Fear/Anxiety

Trembling, hiding, reduced activity, escape behaviors, lowered body posture, dilated pupils.

Frustration

Repetitive behaviors, vocal complaints, pacing, destructive behavior, attention-seeking.

Pain/Discomfort

Limping, reluctance to move, changes in appetite, vocalizing when touched, abnormal posture.

Complex Social Emotions

Jealousy/Competition

Pushing between owner and another pet, attention-seeking when another is being petted, resource guarding.

Grief/Loss

Changes in appetite, lethargy, vocalizing (searching), changes in sleep patterns, loss of interest.

Curiosity/Interest

Forward ears, focused attention, slow approach, sniffing/investigating, head tilting.

Test Your Knowledge

How well can you interpret animal behavior?

1

A dog is licking its lips when no food is present. What does this usually mean?

This is a calming signal indicating stress or anxiety.

2

A cat is slowly blinking while making eye contact with you. What should you do?

Slowly blink back - it's a sign of trust and affection.

3

A rabbit is thumping its hind leg loudly. What is it communicating?

Warning of perceived danger - it's an alarm signal.
