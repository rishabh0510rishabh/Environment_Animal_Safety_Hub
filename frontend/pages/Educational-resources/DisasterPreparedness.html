Disaster Preparedness for Pets

Learn how to keep your pets safe during natural disasters and emergencies. Essential planning and preparation for pet owners to ensure no pet is left behind.

60% of households have pets
1M+ pets affected annually
24-48h Emergency kit needed

Pet Emergency Kit Essentials

Prepare these essential items before disaster strikes. Your pet's survival may depend on it.

Medical Supplies

Essential medical items for pet first aid and ongoing care

  • Pet first aid kit
  • 7-day supply of medications
  • Vaccination records
  • Veterinarian contact information
  • Pet insurance information

Food & Water

Critical supplies to keep your pet nourished and hydrated

  • 3-day supply of dry food (in airtight container)
  • 3-day supply of water (1 gallon per pet per day)
  • Collapsible food/water bowls
  • Manual can opener for canned food
  • Treats for comfort

Safety & Comfort

Items to keep your pet safe, comfortable, and identifiable

  • Sturdy leash, harness, and carrier
  • Current photos of you with your pet
  • Microchip information
  • Comfort items (favorite toy, blanket)
  • Pet waste cleanup supplies

Documents & Information

Important paperwork in waterproof container

  • Pet's medical records (in waterproof bag)
  • Proof of ownership documents
  • Emergency contact list
  • List of pet-friendly shelters/hotels
  • Emergency evacuation plan

Disaster-Specific Preparedness

Different disasters require different preparations. Learn what to do for each type.

Wildfire Preparedness

Wildfires can spread rapidly with little warning. Being prepared is crucial for pet safety.

Before the Fire

Create defensible space around your home. Keep pets indoors when air quality is poor. Practice evacuation drills.

During Evacuation

Leave early - don't wait for mandatory orders. Bring all pets with you. Use carriers for safe transport.

After the Fire

Check for hot spots before letting pets outside. Watch for respiratory issues from smoke inhalation.

Flood Preparedness

Floods can occur quickly, giving you little time to prepare. Early action saves lives.

Before Flooding

Identify high ground in your home. Keep pet carriers accessible. Have life jackets for pets who can swim.

During Flood

Never leave pets chained outside. Bring pets to upper floors. Avoid walking pets in floodwaters.

After Flood

Watch for contaminated water. Check for injuries from debris. Dry pets thoroughly to prevent hypothermia.

Hurricane Preparedness

Hurricanes give advance warning - use this time wisely to prepare your pets.

Before the Storm

Evacuate early if in evacuation zone. Fill prescriptions in advance. Update pet identification.

During the Storm

Keep pets in interior rooms. Use carriers for safety. Have comfort items to reduce stress.

After the Storm

Wait for official all-clear. Keep pets leashed amid debris. Watch for displaced wildlife.

Earthquake Preparedness

Earthquakes strike without warning. Preparation is your best defense.

Before Earthquake

Secure heavy furniture. Store emergency supplies. Practice "drop, cover, hold" with pets.

During Earthquake

Protect yourself first. Call pets to you. Stay away from windows and heavy objects.

After Earthquake

Check for injuries. Leash pets before going outside. Beware of aftershocks and gas leaks.

Tornado Preparedness

Tornadoes can form quickly with little warning. Have a plan ready.

Before Tornado

Identify safe room or basement. Keep pet carriers ready. Have weather radio for alerts.

During Tornado

Go to your safe room immediately. Bring pets in carriers. Protect with blankets or mattresses.

After Tornado

Watch for debris when exiting. Keep pets leashed. Be aware of downed power lines.

Evacuation Plan Step-by-Step

Follow these steps to ensure safe evacuation with your pets during emergencies.

1

Pre-Evacuation Preparation

Keep emergency kit updated and accessible. Ensure pet carriers are ready and labeled. Have current photos of you with your pets.

2

Identification & Documentation

Ensure all pets have proper ID tags. Microchip information should be current. Prepare waterproof document bag with medical records.

3

Transportation Planning

Plan how you'll transport all pets. Have carriers for each animal. Practice loading pets into your vehicle quickly.

4

Shelter Identification

Research pet-friendly shelters and hotels. Have multiple options in different directions. Keep addresses and phone numbers in your kit.

5

Practice & Drills

Conduct regular evacuation drills. Time how long it takes to gather pets and supplies. Update plan based on drill results.
