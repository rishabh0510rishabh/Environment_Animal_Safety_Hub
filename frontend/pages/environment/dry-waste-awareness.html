Understanding Waste

What is Dry Waste?

Dry waste refers to non-biodegradable waste that doesn't decompose easily. These materials can typically be recycled, reused, or repurposed.

Non-Biodegradable

Dry waste doesn't decompose naturally and can take hundreds of years to break down in landfills.

Recyclable Materials

Most dry waste can be processed and turned into new products through recycling facilities.

Not Contaminated

Dry waste should be free from food residue, liquids, or other contaminants to be properly recycled.

Best Practices

Proper Segregation Guide

Follow these steps to properly segregate your dry waste at home.

Step-by-Step Segregation

  • Use separate bins for different types of dry waste
  • Clean and dry all recyclable materials before disposal
  • Remove non-recyclable components (plastic windows from envelopes)
  • Flatten cardboard boxes to save space
  • Keep e-waste separate for special disposal
  • Store segregated waste in labeled containers
Environmental Impact

Benefits of Recycling Dry Waste

Recycling dry waste has numerous environmental and economic benefits.

Saves Resources

Recycling reduces the need for raw materials, preserving natural resources and forests.

Reduces Energy

Recycling uses less energy than producing new products from raw materials.

Creates Jobs

The recycling industry creates employment opportunities in collection, processing, and manufacturing.
