Understanding Climate Change

The science behind what you see in our AR experience

Sea Level Rise

Global sea levels have risen about 8 inches since 1880, with projections of 1-3 feet by 2100. This threatens coastal cities, displacing millions and destroying ecosystems.

  • 680 million people live in low-lying coastal zones
  • Small island nations face existential threat
  • Saltwater intrusion affects freshwater supplies
What You Can Do:

Reduce carbon footprint, support renewable energy, advocate for climate policies

Air Pollution

Air pollution kills 7 million people annually. Cities with AQI above 300 experience severe visibility reduction and health emergencies.

  • 9 out of 10 people breathe polluted air
  • Causes respiratory and cardiovascular diseases
  • Contributes to climate change acceleration
What You Can Do:

Use public transport, reduce energy consumption, plant trees

Deforestation

We lose 10 million hectares of forest annually—equivalent to 27 football fields every minute. Forests absorb 30% of our CO2 emissions.

  • Tropical forests could disappear in 100 years
  • 80% of terrestrial species live in forests
  • Indigenous communities depend on forest ecosystems
What You Can Do:

Support reforestation, choose sustainable products, reduce paper waste

Global Warming

Earth's average temperature has increased by 1.1°C since pre-industrial times. A 2°C rise would cause catastrophic and irreversible changes.

  • More frequent and intense heatwaves
  • Melting ice caps and glaciers
  • Disrupted weather patterns globally
What You Can Do:

Reduce emissions, eat sustainable, vote for climate action
