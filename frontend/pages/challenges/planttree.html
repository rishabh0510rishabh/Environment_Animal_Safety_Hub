Frequently Asked Questions

I don't have a garden. Can I still participate?
Absolutely! Many participants use pots on balconies, patios, or windowsills. Dwarf fruit trees, small ornamental trees, or even bonsai trees are excellent options for small spaces. You can also volunteer to plant trees in community spaces.
What if my tree doesn't survive the 30 days?
Tree planting is a learning experience. If your first attempt doesn't succeed, you can try again with a new tree or different approach. Our community shares tips to improve survival rates. The important thing is participating and learning.
How much time does daily care require?
For most trees, daily care involves just checking soil moisture and ensuring adequate water. This typically takes 5-10 minutes per day. Weekly, you might spend 15-20 minutes checking for pests or providing nutrients.
Is 20kg of CO₂ really saved by one tree in 30 days?
Yes, a young tree can absorb approximately 5-6kg of CO₂ per year, which averages to about 20kg over its first 30 days of active growth when properly cared for. As the tree matures, it will absorb significantly more - up to 22kg per year when fully grown.
