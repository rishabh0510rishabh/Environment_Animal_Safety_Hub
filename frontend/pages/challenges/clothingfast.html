The Fast Fashion Problem

Water Waste

The fashion industry uses 93 billion cubic meters of water annually - enough for 5 million people's consumption. A single cotton t-shirt requires 2,700 liters of water to produce.

Carbon Footprint

Fashion accounts for 10% of global carbon emissions - more than international flights and maritime shipping combined. Fast fashion garments are worn only 7 times on average.

Clothing Waste

85% of textiles end up in landfills each year. The equivalent of one garbage truck of clothes is burned or dumped every second globally.

Human Impact

Fast fashion relies on cheap labor, with many workers earning less than $3/day. The industry employs 1 in 6 people globally, often in poor working conditions.

Sustainable Alternatives

During your 30-day fast, explore these sustainable fashion practices instead of shopping

Clothing Swap

Organize or join clothing swaps with friends to refresh your wardrobe without buying new items.

Upcycle & Repair

Learn basic mending skills or transform old clothes into new pieces through creative upcycling.

Secondhand First

After the fast, prioritize thrift stores, consignment shops, and online secondhand platforms.

Closet Audit

Rediscover forgotten items in your closet and create new outfits with what you already own.

Capsule Wardrobe Tips

Create a versatile wardrobe with fewer, higher-quality pieces

The 30-Wear Test

Before buying any clothing item, ask yourself: Will I wear this at least 30 times? If not, reconsider the purchase.

Quality Over Quantity

Invest in well-made, timeless pieces that will last for years rather than trendy items that quickly wear out.

Mix & Match

Build your wardrobe around complementary colors and styles that can be mixed to create multiple outfits.

Seasonal Rotation

Store off-season clothing to keep your closet manageable and rediscover items when seasons change.
