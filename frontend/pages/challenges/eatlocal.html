Benefits of Eating Local

Reduce Food Miles

The average meal travels 1,500 miles to reach your plate. Local food travels less than 100 miles, reducing transportation emissions by 95%.

Fresher, More Nutritious

Local food is harvested at peak ripeness and reaches you faster, retaining more nutrients and flavor compared to transported produce.

Support Local Economy

For every $100 spent at a local farm, $68 stays in the local economy compared to $43 at chain stores.

Seasonal Eating

Eating with the seasons connects you to natural cycles, reduces energy for storage, and provides variety throughout the year.

How to Eat Local

Farmers Markets

Visit weekly farmers markets for fresh, seasonal produce directly from growers.

CSA Subscriptions

Join a Community Supported Agriculture program for regular boxes of local produce.

Local Food Co-ops

Shop at cooperatives that prioritize locally-sourced products from regional producers.

Local Food Directories

Use apps and websites to find farms, markets, and restaurants serving local food.
