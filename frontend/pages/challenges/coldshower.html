How It Works

  1. Take your normal warm shower - Cleanse and relax as you normally would
  2. Gradually reduce temperature - Slowly turn the dial to cooler water
  3. 30 seconds of cold water - End your shower with 30 seconds of cold water (as cold as you can handle)
  4. Track your progress - Log each day and note how you feel
  5. Build up over time - Try increasing cold exposure by 5 seconds each day
  6. Join the community - Share your experiences and get support

Pro Tip

Focus on your breathing when the cold water hits. Take deep, controlled breaths to manage the initial shock. It gets easier with practice!

Frequently Asked Questions

Is 30 seconds of cold water really enough to see benefits?
Yes! Research shows that even brief cold exposure can stimulate the immune system and increase alertness. The 30-second timeframe makes the challenge accessible while still providing benefits.
Can I do this if I have health concerns?
If you have heart conditions, circulation issues, or are pregnant, consult your doctor before starting. For most people, ending a shower with cool water is safe, but listen to your body.
What's the best time of day for a cold shower?
Most participants prefer morning showers to boost energy for the day. However, you can adapt this to your schedule. Some find an evening cool-down shower helps with sleep.
How much energy does this actually save?
Reducing your hot shower time by 30 seconds daily can save approximately 5kg of CO₂ over 14 days. If continued for a year, that's over 130kg of CO₂ saved per person!
