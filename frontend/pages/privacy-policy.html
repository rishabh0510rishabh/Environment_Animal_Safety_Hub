Official Privacy Policy

Your Privacy Matters to Us

At EcoLife, we believe transparency builds trust. This document explains how we collect, use, and protect your information while you explore our environmental conservation platform.

Information We Collect

EcoLife collects minimal data necessary for educational purposes. This includes anonymous usage statistics, quiz responses for educational insights, and voluntary form submissions. We never collect sensitive personal information without explicit consent.

How We Use Information

Data helps us improve content quality, personalize learning experiences, and understand which conservation topics interest our community most. All analytics are aggregated and anonymized to protect individual privacy.

Cookies & Tracking

We use essential cookies for website functionality. Optional analytics cookies help us improve user experience. You have full control over cookie preferences and can adjust settings at any time through your browser.

External Links

Our platform may link to partner organizations, educational resources, or conservation initiatives. While we vet these links, we encourage reviewing their privacy policies as they operate independently from EcoLife.

Children's Privacy

EcoLife is designed to be family-friendly. We comply with COPPA regulations and do not knowingly collect data from children under 13 without parental consent. Our content is educational and safe for all ages.

Policy Updates

We regularly review and update our privacy practices. Significant changes will be communicated through our platform. Continued use of EcoLife after updates constitutes acceptance of the revised policy.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is my data sold to third parties?
No. EcoLife does not sell, rent, or trade user data. We believe in ethical data practices that prioritize user privacy above all else.
How can I delete my data?
Contact us at privacy@ecolife.org with your request. We'll process deletion requests within 30 days and confirm completion.
Do you use location tracking?
Only with explicit permission for location-based features like local wildlife information. You can disable location services at any time.
