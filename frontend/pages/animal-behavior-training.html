Expert Tips

1

Consistency is Key

Use the same commands and rewards every time. Inconsistency confuses pets and slows learning.

2

Keep Sessions Short

5-10 minute sessions are more effective than long, tiring ones. Multiple short sessions work best.

3

Positive Reinforcement

Reward good behavior immediately. Pets learn faster with praise and treats than with punishment.

4

Read Body Language

Learn to understand your pet's signals. A stressed pet can't learn effectively.

5

Be Patient

Every pet learns at their own pace. Celebrate small victories and stay positive.

6

Practice Daily

Regular practice reinforces learning. Make training a fun part of your daily routine.

Sample Training Schedule

A 4-week beginner training plan to get you started

Week 1: Foundation

  • Learn basic commands (sit, stay, come)
  • Introduce positive reinforcement
  • Establish routine and consistency
  • Practice 2-3 times daily, 5-10 minutes each

Week 2: Building Skills

  • Add "down" and "leave it" commands
  • Practice in different environments
  • Introduce leash training basics
  • Increase session length to 10-15 minutes

Week 3: Advanced Basics

  • Teach "heel" and loose-leash walking
  • Work on impulse control
  • Practice commands with distractions
  • Continue daily practice sessions

Week 4: Refinement

  • Perfect all learned commands
  • Add duration to commands (longer stays)
  • Practice in challenging situations
  • Begin introducing fun tricks
