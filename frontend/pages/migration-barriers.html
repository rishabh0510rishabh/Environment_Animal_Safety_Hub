The Connectivity Crisis

Animal migration is one of nature's most spectacular phenomena, yet human infrastructure increasingly fragments habitats and disrupts these ancient pathways. Roads, fences, and urban development create barriers that prevent animals from accessing essential resources.

Types of Migration Barriers

Roads and Highways

The most pervasive barrier to wildlife movement. High-speed traffic creates lethal obstacles and psychological barriers that animals avoid.

Causes 1 million+ animal deaths annually in the US

Fences and Barriers

Agricultural, security, and property fences prevent movement across landscapes, especially for large mammals and herding species.

Blocks migration routes for species like pronghorn and elk

Urban Development

Cities and suburbs create permanent barriers and alter landscapes, forcing animals into smaller, isolated habitat patches.

Reduces habitat connectivity by up to 90% in urban areas

Agricultural Expansion

Intensive farming creates monoculture landscapes that lack the connectivity needed for wildlife movement.

Converts 24 million acres of habitat annually worldwide

Water Infrastructure

Dams, canals, and water diversions block aquatic migration routes and alter river ecosystems.

Affects 40% of freshwater fish species globally

Energy Infrastructure

Power lines, pipelines, and renewable energy installations create both physical and sensory barriers.

Wind farms can disrupt bat and bird migration patterns

Impact on Wildlife Species

Large Mammals

Species like elephants, bison, and caribou require vast territories for migration. Barriers force them into smaller areas, increasing competition and stress.

African elephants, American bison, Mongolian gazelles

Birds

Migratory birds face obstacles from tall structures and urban lights that disrupt navigation and resting patterns.

Whooping cranes, monarch butterflies, songbirds

Amphibians

Roads create lethal barriers for slow-moving amphibians attempting to reach breeding ponds.

Wood frogs, spotted salamanders, toads

Aquatic Species

Dams and water diversions prevent fish from reaching spawning grounds and alter river ecosystems.

Salmon, eels, sturgeon, freshwater mussels

Success Stories

Banff National Park, Canada

Challenge: Trans-Canada Highway bisected Banff, blocking wildlife movement.

Solution: 38 wildlife crossings including overpasses and underpasses.

90% reduction in wildlife-vehicle collisions, restored connectivity for 13 species

Yellowstone to Yukon, North America

Challenge: Fragmented habitats across international borders.

Solution: Y2Y conservation initiative creating wildlife corridors.

Largest ecological network in Europe, connecting habitats across 24 countries
