Subtropical Deserts

Located near the Tropic of Cancer and Capricorn, these are the hottest and driest deserts on Earth, formed by persistent high-pressure systems that prevent cloud formation.

Location

20-30° latitude, both hemispheres

Temperature

Average: 25-40°C, Extreme: up to 58°C

Precipitation

<250mm/year, often less than 25mm

Famous Examples

Sahara Desert

World's largest hot desert, covering 9.2 million km² across North Africa

58°C record 25mm/year
Arabian Desert

Rub' al Khali (Empty Quarter) contains the largest continuous sand desert

2.3M km² 50mm/year
Australian Deserts

Great Sandy, Gibson, and Great Victoria deserts cover most of Australia

70% continent 150mm/year
