Scientific Evidence

Ocean Current Reorganization

Global ocean circulation patterns are undergoing dramatic changes, with major current systems weakening and shifting in response to climate change.

Gulf Stream Weakening

The Gulf Stream has weakened by 15-20% since the 1950s, disrupting larval transport across the Atlantic Ocean basin.

Pacific Current Shifts

The California Current has shifted northward by 50-100 km, affecting larval delivery to traditional settlement habitats.

Global Current Slowdown

Surface current speeds have decreased by 10-30% in many regions, extending larval drift times and increasing mortality.

Larval Dispersal Consequences

Disrupted current patterns have severe implications for marine species that rely on larval dispersal for population replenishment.

  • Recruitment Failure: Marine species show 40-80% declines in juvenile settlement success
  • Population Isolation: Reduced gene flow between populations leads to genetic bottlenecks
  • Range Shift Mismatches: Adult populations shift poleward while larvae fail to reach new areas
  • Ecosystem Connectivity Loss: Breakdown of marine population networks affects biodiversity
