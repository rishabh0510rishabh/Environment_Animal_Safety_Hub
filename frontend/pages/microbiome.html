What is the Gut Microbiome?

Understanding the complex microbial community living in animal digestive systems

Microbial Ecosystem

Symbiotic Relationship
"The gut microbiome is a complex community of trillions of microorganisms, including bacteria, archaea, viruses, and fungi, that live in the digestive tracts of animals. This microbial ecosystem plays crucial roles in digestion, immunity, and overall health." - National Institutes of Health
