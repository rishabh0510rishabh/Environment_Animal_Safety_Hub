Vultures and other scavenger species provide essential ecosystem services by rapidly removing carcasses, preventing the spread of diseases. Their dramatic population declines have created a perfect storm for pathogen proliferation, threatening wildlife, livestock, and human health across the globe.

Disease Risk Amplification

When scavengers disappear, carcasses persist much longer, becoming breeding grounds for pathogens and attracting inefficient scavengers that spread disease rather than containing it.

â° Carcass Persistence

Without vultures, carcasses remain for weeks, allowing pathogens to multiply and spread to new hosts through flies, rodents, and feral dogs.
