Switch to LED Bulbs

Easy

Replace traditional incandescent bulbs with energy-efficient LED bulbs to reduce electricity consumption and save money on utility bills.

How to do it:

  1. Check current bulb wattage and type
  2. Buy equivalent LED bulbs (use 75% less energy)
  3. Replace bulbs one room at a time
  4. Dispose of old bulbs properly at recycling centers
Environmental Impact:

Save 80% on lighting energy costs and reduce 1,400 lbs of CO₂ emissions per year

Fix Water Leaks

Easy

A single dripping faucet can waste over 3,000 gallons of water per year. Fix leaks promptly to conserve water and reduce bills.

How to do it:

  1. Check all faucets, toilets, and pipes for leaks
  2. Replace worn washers and gaskets
  3. Use food coloring in toilet tank to detect hidden leaks
  4. Call a plumber for major leaks
Environmental Impact:

Save up to 10% on water bills and conserve thousands of gallons annually

Start Composting

Medium

Turn kitchen scraps and yard waste into nutrient-rich compost for your garden while reducing landfill waste.

How to do it:

  1. Choose a compost bin or create a pile in your yard
  2. Add brown materials (leaves, paper) and green materials (food scraps)
  3. Turn the pile weekly and keep it moist
  4. Use finished compost in 3-6 months
Environmental Impact:

Reduce household waste by 30% and create free fertilizer for plants

Bike or Walk Short Trips

Easy

Replace car trips under 2 miles with walking or biking. It's healthier for you and the environment.

How to do it:

  1. Plan errands to combine multiple stops
  2. Use bike lanes and safe walking routes
  3. Invest in a good bike and safety gear
  4. Start with 1-2 trips per week and increase gradually
Environmental Impact:

Reduce CO₂ emissions by 2,400 lbs per year and improve personal health

Eat More Plant-Based Meals

Medium

Reduce meat consumption by having plant-based meals 2-3 times per week to lower your carbon footprint.

How to do it:

  1. Start with "Meatless Monday" once a week
  2. Try plant-based proteins like beans, lentils, tofu
  3. Explore vegetarian recipes and cuisines
  4. Gradually increase to 3-4 plant-based meals per week
Environmental Impact:

Reduce water usage by 200 gallons and CO₂ by 1,900 lbs per year

Adjust Thermostat Settings

Easy

Lower heating by 2°F in winter and raise cooling by 2°F in summer to save energy without sacrificing comfort.

How to do it:

  1. Set winter temperature to 68°F when home, 65°F when away
  2. Set summer temperature to 78°F when home, 85°F when away
  3. Use programmable or smart thermostat for automation
  4. Wear appropriate clothing for the season
Environmental Impact:

Save 10-15% on heating and cooling costs, reduce CO₂ by 2,000 lbs annually

Unplug Electronics When Not in Use

Easy

Many electronics consume "phantom power" even when turned off. Unplug devices to eliminate this waste.

How to do it:

  1. Use power strips for easy unplugging of multiple devices
  2. Unplug chargers when not charging devices
  3. Focus on high-consumption items like TVs, computers, printers
  4. Consider smart power strips that cut phantom power automatically
Environmental Impact:

Reduce electricity usage by 5-10% and save $100+ annually on energy bills

Take Shorter Showers

Easy

Reduce shower time by just 2 minutes to save water and energy used for heating water.

How to do it:

  1. Set a timer for 5-7 minutes
  2. Turn off water while soaping or shampooing
  3. Install a low-flow showerhead
  4. Play a short song as your shower timer
Environmental Impact:

Save 2,500 gallons of water and $35 in energy costs per year
