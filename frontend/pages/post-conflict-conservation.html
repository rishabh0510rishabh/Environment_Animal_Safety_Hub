Human-Wildlife Dynamics

Major Challenges

Obstacles that complicate conservation efforts in post-conflict regions

Landmines & UXO

Unexploded ordnance and landmines contaminate vast areas, preventing access for conservation work and endangering wildlife and humans.

110 million landmines worldwide

Illegal Poaching

Weak law enforcement and economic desperation drive increased poaching of endangered species for bushmeat and illegal trade.

20,000 elephants poached annually

Infrastructure Damage

Destroyed roads, bridges, and facilities hinder monitoring efforts and anti-poaching patrols in remote areas.

$100B+ in reconstruction needed

Population Displacement

Mass displacement creates refugee camps and informal settlements that encroach on wildlife habitats.

80 million displaced people

Corruption & Weak Governance

Corrupt officials and weak institutions enable illegal logging, mining, and wildlife trafficking.

$7-10B illegal wildlife trade

Climate Change Vulnerability

Post-conflict ecosystems are less resilient to climate change impacts like drought and extreme weather.

30% more vulnerable to climate events

Success Stories

Inspiring examples of conservation success in post-conflict regions

Mozambique's Gorongosa National Park

After 15 years of civil war, the park lost 90% of its wildlife. A $40 million restoration project has brought back elephants, lions, and zebras through community-based conservation and ecotourism.

Following the 1994 genocide, gorilla populations rebounded from 250 to over 1,000 individuals through intensive protection and community tourism programs that provide economic benefits to local villages.

Sierra Leone's Western Area Peninsula

After a decade of civil war, chimpanzee populations were on the brink. Conservation efforts combined mine clearance, community education, and sustainable agriculture to create a 75,000-acre national park.

International Cooperation

Post-conflict reconstruction attracts global aid and expertise, providing resources for comprehensive conservation programs.
