Health Records Tips

  • Keep copies of all vaccination certificates
  • Store medical history and test results
  • Document any chronic conditions or treatments
  • Take photos of any skin conditions or injuries
  • Keep receipts for medical expenses (for insurance)
  • Update microchip registration with current contact info
  • Have pet insurance policy details readily available

General Health & Wellness Tips

Nutrition

  • Feed high-quality, age-appropriate food
  • Maintain consistent feeding schedule
  • Monitor portion sizes to prevent obesity
  • Provide fresh water at all times
  • Avoid toxic foods (chocolate, grapes, onions, etc.)

Exercise

  • Daily physical activity appropriate for species/breed
  • Mental stimulation through play and training
  • Adjust exercise based on age and health
  • Watch for signs of overexertion
  • Provide safe outdoor spaces for exploration

Grooming

  • Regular brushing to prevent matting
  • Trim nails to prevent overgrowth
  • Clean ears regularly to prevent infections
  • Dental care (brushing, dental treats)
  • Check for parasites (fleas, ticks)

Preventive Care

  • Annual veterinary checkups
  • Keep vaccinations current
  • Regular parasite prevention (heartworm, fleas, ticks)
  • Spay/neuter when appropriate
  • Monitor for behavioral changes

Emergency Warning Signs

Seek immediate veterinary care if you notice any of these symptoms:

Difficulty breathing or rapid breathing
Severe vomiting or diarrhea (especially with blood)
Collapse or extreme lethargy
Seizures or loss of consciousness
Inability to urinate or defecate
Signs of poisoning (drooling, tremors, weakness)
Eye injuries or sudden blindness
Suspected broken bones or severe injuries
