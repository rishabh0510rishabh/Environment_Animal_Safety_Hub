What Are Animal Sanctuaries?

Animal sanctuaries are specialized facilities dedicated to providing lifetime care for animals that cannot survive in the wild due to injury, captivity, or human encroachment. Unlike zoos or wildlife rehabilitation centers, sanctuaries focus on permanent care rather than release.

ğŸ  Sanctuary vs. Zoo

  • No Breeding Programs: Sanctuaries do not breed animals for conservation or profit
  • No Public Displays: Animals are not exhibited for entertainment
  • Rescue-Focused: Primary mission is providing care for rescued individuals
  • Ethical Standards: Commitment to animal welfare above all else

ğŸ¦ Types of Sanctuaries

  • Species-Specific: Dedicated to one type of animal (elephant sanctuaries, primate sanctuaries)
  • Multi-Species: Care for various animals in one facility
  • Regional: Focus on local wildlife and conservation issues
  • International: Address global conservation challenges
