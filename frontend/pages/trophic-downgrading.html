The Silent Collapse of Ecosystems

When apex predators disappear, the effects ripple through entire food webs. Herbivores multiply unchecked, vegetation disappears, and ecosystems transform in ways that can take generations to reverse. This trophic downgrading represents one of the most profound threats to biodiversity and ecosystem function.
