When to Report Wildlife Incidents

Understanding different types of wildlife reports and when action is needed

Wildlife Sightings

Report rare or endangered species sightings to help conservation efforts

Priority: Medium
  • Take photos from safe distance
  • Note location and time
  • Report to local wildlife agency
  • Do not disturb the animal

Injured Animals

Animals showing signs of injury, illness, or distress need immediate attention

Priority: Critical
  • Do not approach dangerous animals
  • Call wildlife rescue immediately
  • Provide exact location
  • Stay at safe distance

Illegal Activities

Poaching, trapping, habitat destruction, and other wildlife crimes

Priority: Critical
  • Do not confront suspects
  • Take photos/video if safe
  • Report to law enforcement
  • Provide detailed descriptions

Orphaned Young

Baby animals that appear to be abandoned or orphaned

Priority: High
  • Observe from distance first
  • Check for parent nearby
  • Contact wildlife rehabilitator
  • Do not feed or touch

üìã How to Make an Effective Report

Follow these guidelines to ensure your wildlife report is useful and actionable

1

Stay Safe First

Never put yourself in danger. Observe from a safe distance and do not approach wildlife.

2

Gather Information

Note exact location, time, date, and detailed description of what you observed.

3

Take Photos/Video

If safe, document the incident with photos or video, but prioritize safety over evidence.

4

Contact the Right Agency

Use the appropriate emergency or non-emergency contact based on the situation's urgency.

5

Follow Up

Provide any additional information requested and follow up if you have more details.

üõ°Ô∏è Safety Tips for Wildlife Reporters

  • Keep Distance: Maintain at least 100 feet from large wildlife, 25 feet from small animals
  • Don't Feed: Never feed wildlife - it can alter natural behavior and spread disease
  • Secure Pets: Keep dogs leashed and cats indoors when reporting incidents
  • Respect Habitat: Stay on trails and don't disturb natural areas
  • Report Anonymously: If concerned about retaliation, most agencies accept anonymous reports
