Initial Assessment

First steps when encountering injured wildlife

Ensure Safety First

Protect yourself and others before approaching. Wear gloves and avoid direct contact with unknown animals.

Observe from Distance

Assess the situation without getting too close. Look for signs of injury, consciousness, and immediate threats.

Call Professionals

Contact local wildlife rehabilitation centers, animal control, or emergency services immediately.

Common Emergency Situations

Specific protocols for different types of wildlife emergencies

Road Accidents

1

Stop traffic safely and create a barrier around the animal

2

Do not move the animal unless it's in immediate danger

3

Cover with a blanket to reduce stress and protect from elements

4

Call wildlife rescue immediately - provide exact location

Snake Bites & Venomous Encounters

1

Keep the animal calm and restrict movement to slow venom spread

2

Do NOT attempt to suck out venom or apply tourniquets

3

Keep the bite area below heart level if possible

4

Seek immediate veterinary attention - time is critical

Physical Trauma

1

Control bleeding with clean cloth or gauze (direct pressure)

2

Immobilize broken limbs with splints if safe to do so

3

Keep animal warm but not overheated

4

Transport carefully to wildlife rehabilitation center

Orphaned Wildlife

1

Observe from a distance - parents may be nearby

2

Do not feed or give water unless instructed by experts

3

Keep warm in a quiet, dark place

4

Contact wildlife rehabilitator for proper care

Stabilization Techniques

How to safely stabilize injured wildlife before professional help arrives

Containment

  • Use appropriate carriers (cardboard boxes for small animals)
  • Line containers with soft materials
  • Ensure adequate ventilation
  • Avoid wire cages that can cause further injury

Temperature Control

  • Keep warm-blooded animals warm (not hot)
  • Keep cold-blooded animals at appropriate temperatures
  • Use heating pads wrapped in towels
  • Avoid direct heat sources

Transport Safety

  • Drive slowly and avoid sudden stops
  • Keep containers secure in vehicle
  • Have destination phone number ready
  • Call ahead to prepare receiving facility

Prevention is Key

Reduce wildlife emergencies through proactive measures

Drive Responsibly

Slow down in wildlife areas, especially at dawn and dusk when animals are most active.

Secure Property

Keep garbage secured and remove attractants that draw wildlife into dangerous areas.

Protect Habitats

Support conservation efforts that reduce human-wildlife conflicts.

Educate Others

Share knowledge about wildlife safety to prevent emergencies.
