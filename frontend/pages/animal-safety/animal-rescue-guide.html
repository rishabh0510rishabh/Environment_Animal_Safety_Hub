Animal Rescue Guide

A comprehensive step-by-step guide on how to safely assist injured or distressed wildlife while ensuring your safety and the animal's well-being.

Step 1: Assess the Situation (S.T.O.P.)

S - Stop & Observe

Do not approach immediately. Watch from a distance. Is the animal actually injured, or is it a fledgling learning to fly or a baby waiting for a parent?

T - Think Safety

Are you in a safe location? Is there traffic? Is the animal dangerous (e.g., raptors, large mammals, snakes)? Never put yourself in danger.

O - Observe Options

Does it need rescue? Visible blood, drooping wing, limping, fly-strike (flies buzzing around), or unresponsive behavior are signs it needs help.

P - Plan & Phone

Before touching, have a plan. Prepare a box. Call a wildlife rehabilitator for advice if you are unsure.

Step 2: Safety First

  • Wear Gloves: Heavy leather gloves for mammals/raptors; latex gloves for amphibians/small birds to protect them from your oils and you from diseases.
  • Protect Your Eyes: Some birds (herons, loons) attack eyes. Wear glasses or sunglasses.
  • Do Not Pet: Stress kills wildlife. They do not find comfort in human touch.
  • Do Not Feed: Giving food or water can be fatal if the animal is in shock or cold.
RABIES VECTOR SPECIES: Be extremely careful with raccoons, skunks, bats, foxes, and groundhogs. Do not touch without thick protection. If bitten, seek medical help immediately.

Handling & Containment Techniques

Birds

Approach from behind. Gently drape a towel over the bird covering the head (darkness calms them). Scoop up holding the wings against the body.

Watch out for beaks and talons!

Small Mammals

Use a thick towel or heavy gloves. Cover the head. Grasp securely around the shoulders/neck to prevent biting. Place quickly in a box.

Turtles

Hold the shell securely with both hands near the back legs. NEVER lift by the tail. For snapping turtles, slide on a shovel or mat; their neck reach is long!

Step 3: Secure & Transport

  1. Prepare a sturdy cardboard box with air holes (poke them BEFORE putting the animal in).
  2. Line it with an old T-shirt or towel. No raveling terry cloth (toes can get stuck).
  3. Place the animal inside and tape the box shut securely.
  4. Keep it Warm, Dark, and Quiet. No radio, no talking, no AC blasting directly on it.
  5. Do not peek. Each look causes stress.

Step 4: Contact Professionals

It is illegal to keep wildlife in most places. You must get them to a licensed rehabilitator.
