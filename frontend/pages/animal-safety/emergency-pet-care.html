Emergency Kit

Assemble a pet first aid kit with essential supplies for emergencies.

  • Gauze bandages and tape
  • Antiseptic wipes
  • Hydrogen peroxide (3%)
  • Digital thermometer
  • Muzzle and leash
  • Blanket and towel
  • Plastic bags for waste

Pet Information

Keep important information about your pet easily accessible.

  • Medical records and vaccination history
  • Current medications and dosages
  • Allergies and medical conditions
  • Microchip number and registration
  • Recent photos for identification
  • Feeding schedule and diet information

Common Pet Emergencies

Recognize the signs and know how to respond to these critical situations.

Poisoning

Signs: Vomiting, diarrhea, drooling, seizures, difficulty breathing

Action: Call poison control immediately. Do not induce vomiting unless instructed.

Pet Poison Helpline: (855) 764-7661

Trauma/Injury

Signs: Bleeding, limping, swelling, difficulty breathing, unconsciousness

Action: Control bleeding, keep pet warm and still, transport carefully to vet.

Do: Apply pressure to bleeding wounds
Don't: Give pain medication without vet approval

Heat Stroke

Signs: Excessive panting, drooling, weakness, collapse, bright red gums

Action: Move to cool area, apply cool (not cold) water, offer small amounts of water.

Prevention: Never leave pets in hot cars
Warning: Heat stroke can kill in minutes

Medication Issues

Signs: Vomiting, lethargy, tremors, seizures, difficulty breathing

Action: Contact vet immediately. Bring medication bottle/container.

Note: Human medications can be toxic to pets
Common culprits: Pain relievers, antidepressants, cold medicine

Choking

Signs: Difficulty breathing, pawing at mouth, blue gums, unconsciousness

Action: Perform Heimlich maneuver or seek immediate veterinary help.

For dogs: Stand behind, make fist, thrust upward
For cats: Hold upside down, compress chest

Seizures

Signs: Convulsions, paddling, loss of consciousness, drooling

Action: Keep pet safe, time the seizure, contact vet immediately.

Don't: Put anything in the mouth
Do: Move away dangerous objects

Pet CPR Basics

Learn the basic steps of cardiopulmonary resuscitation for pets.

Check Responsiveness

Call your pet's name and gently shake their shoulder. Check for breathing and heartbeat.

Call for Help

Have someone call your veterinarian or emergency clinic immediately.

Clear Airway

Check the mouth for obstructions. Remove any visible foreign objects.

Begin CPR

For dogs: Place on right side, compress chest 100-120 times per minute.

For cats: Place on back, compress chest 100-120 times per minute.

Give Rescue Breaths

Close mouth, blow gently into nostrils every 4-5 seconds.

Watch CPR Demonstration

Note: Pet CPR should only be performed by trained individuals. Always seek professional veterinary care immediately.

Prevention is Key

Take these steps to reduce the risk of pet emergencies.

Regular Check-ups

Schedule annual veterinary visits and keep vaccinations current.

Safe Environment

Pet-proof your home and yard to prevent accidents and poisonings.

Identification

Microchip your pet and keep ID tags with current contact information.

Training

Train your pet with basic commands and supervise interactions with other animals.
