What is Wildlife Poaching?

Wildlife poaching is the illegal hunting, killing, or capturing of wild animals, often for profit. This devastating practice threatens biodiversity, disrupts ecosystems, and drives countless species toward extinction. Poaching affects everything from majestic elephants and rhinos to rare birds and marine life.

International Laws

  • CITES (1975): Regulates international trade in endangered species
  • Lacey Act (1900): US law prohibiting trade in illegally taken wildlife
  • Endangered Species Act (1973): US protection for threatened species
  • EU Wildlife Trade Regulations: Controls wildlife imports and exports
  • UN Convention Against Transnational Crime: Addresses organized wildlife crime

Enforcement Agencies

  • INTERPOL: International Criminal Police Organization
  • UNODC: United Nations Office on Drugs and Crime
  • USFWS: US Fish and Wildlife Service
  • Europol: European Union Agency for Law Enforcement
  • National Park Services: Protected area enforcement worldwide

Investigation Techniques
