Common Household & Environmental Poisons

Be aware of these dangerous substances that can harm animals

Human Foods

Chocolate & Caffeine

Contains theobromine and caffeine, toxic to dogs, cats, and many wildlife species

Grapes & Raisins

Can cause kidney failure in dogs

Onions & Garlic

Damages red blood cells, leading to anemia

Avocado

Contains persin, toxic to birds and some mammals

Macadamia Nuts

Causes weakness, tremors, and hyperthermia in dogs

Alcohol

Can cause coma or death in small animals

Household Chemicals

Cleaning Products

Bleach, detergents, and disinfectants can cause burns and organ damage

Antifreeze

Sweet-tasting ethylene glycol causes kidney failure

Rodenticides

Rat and mouse poisons can cause internal bleeding

Pesticides & Insecticides

Can cause neurological damage and seizures

Batteries

Lead and acid can cause severe burns and toxicity

Medications

Human medications like ibuprofen, acetaminophen can be deadly

Toxic Plants

Lilies

Highly toxic to cats, causing kidney failure

Sago Palm

Contains cycasin, toxic to dogs and cats

Oleander

Contains cardiac glycosides, affects heart function

Azalea & Rhododendron

Grayanotoxins cause vomiting, diarrhea, and heart issues

Tulips & Daffodils

Bulbs contain toxic alkaloids

Tomato Plants

Leaves and stems contain solanine

Environmental Toxins

Lead Paint

Causes neurological damage and anemia

Mold & Mildew

Can cause respiratory issues and mycotoxin poisoning

Cigarettes & Tobacco

Nicotine is highly toxic to animals

Motor Oil & Gasoline

Petroleum products cause liver and kidney damage

Fertilizers

Chemical burns and organ damage

Wild Mushrooms

Many species are toxic to animals

Prevention Strategies

Keep your pets and wildlife safe with these essential precautions

Home Safety

  • Store chemicals and medications in locked cabinets
  • Keep trash cans secured and covered
  • Use pet-safe alternatives for cleaning
  • Remove toxic plants from your yard
  • Keep antifreeze in a secure garage

Kitchen Safety

  • Never feed human food without vet approval
  • Keep chocolate and caffeine out of reach
  • Store onions, garlic, and grapes securely
  • Use trash cans with secure lids
  • Clean up food spills immediately

Garden & Outdoor Safety

  • Identify and remove toxic plants
  • Use natural pest control methods
  • Keep pets supervised outdoors
  • Avoid chemical fertilizers near pet areas
  • Provide safe alternatives for chewing

Wildlife Protection

  • Avoid using rodenticides in natural areas
  • Properly dispose of hazardous waste
  • Support organic farming practices
  • Create wildlife corridors
  • Educate others about environmental toxins

Emergency Signs of Poisoning

Recognize these symptoms and seek immediate veterinary care

Gastrointestinal Signs

  • Vomiting
  • Diarrhea
  • Excessive drooling
  • Loss of appetite
  • Abdominal pain

Neurological Signs

  • Seizures or tremors
  • Disorientation or confusion
  • Weakness or paralysis
  • Hyperactivity or restlessness
  • Coma

Cardiovascular Signs

  • Rapid or irregular heartbeat
  • Difficulty breathing
  • Pale gums
  • Collapse
  • Shock

Other Signs

  • Blood in urine or stool
  • Jaundice (yellowing of skin)
  • Burns or chemical damage
  • Swelling or hives
  • Unusual odor from breath

What to Do in Case of Poisoning

  1. Stay calm and remove the animal from the source of poison
  2. Do not induce vomiting unless directed by a veterinarian
  3. Call your veterinarian or emergency clinic immediately
  4. Bring the packaging or substance if possible
  5. Monitor vital signs and be prepared to perform CPR if needed
  6. Contact poison control for animals (ASPCA: (888) 426-4435)

Safe Alternatives

Pet-safe products and practices for a healthy environment

Pet-Safe Foods

  • Carrots and apples (in moderation)
  • Plain cooked chicken or turkey
  • Rice or plain oatmeal
  • Commercial pet treats
  • Fresh vegetables like green beans

Safe Cleaning Products

  • Vinegar and water solutions
  • Baking soda for scrubbing
  • Hydrogen peroxide (diluted)
  • Pet-safe enzymatic cleaners
  • Natural essential oil alternatives

Safe Plants

  • Spider plants
  • African violets
  • Boston ferns
  • Cat grass or wheatgrass
  • Roses (remove thorns)

Natural Pest Control

  • Diatomaceous earth
  • Essential oil sprays (diluted)
  • Beneficial insects
  • Neem oil treatments
  • Physical barriers
