Wildlife Rehabilitation Centers

Specialized facilities dedicated to nursing injured and orphaned wildlife back to health, preparing them for release into their natural habitats.

What Are Wildlife Rehabilitation Centers?

Wildlife rehabilitation centers are licensed facilities that provide temporary care for injured, orphaned, or sick wild animals. These centers employ trained professionals who specialize in wildlife medicine, ensuring that animals receive the proper treatment they need to recover and return to the wild.

Medical Treatment

Professional veterinary care for injuries, illnesses, and rehabilitation from human-related incidents like vehicle collisions or habitat destruction.

Orphan Care

Specialized care for orphaned young animals, including hand-rearing, feeding schedules, and socialization to prepare them for independent life.

Rehabilitation Training

Programs to rebuild strength, teach survival skills, and ensure animals can hunt, forage, and avoid predators before release.

Release Preparation

Careful planning to return animals to appropriate habitats at optimal times, with monitoring to ensure successful reintegration.

How Rehabilitation Centers Help Animals

Vehicle Collision Victims

Animals hit by vehicles often suffer from broken bones, internal injuries, or shock. Rehabilitation centers provide surgical care, pain management, and physical therapy to help them recover.

Oil Spill Cleanup

Birds and marine mammals affected by oil spills receive specialized cleaning, medical treatment, and rehabilitation to restore their natural waterproofing and health.

Orphaned Young

Young animals separated from their parents due to habitat loss or human activity need round-the-clock care, proper nutrition, and learning essential survival skills.

Habitat Destruction

Animals displaced by natural disasters or development projects receive temporary sanctuary while their natural habitats are restored or new ones are found.

Success Stories

Barn Owl Recovery

A barn owl with a broken wing was treated for three months, learning to fly again before successful release back into the wild.

Sea Turtle Rehabilitation

Over 500 sea turtles were treated for fibropapillomatosis, a tumor-causing disease, with many successfully returning to ocean habitats.

Fawn Rescue

An orphaned fawn found alone in a park was bottle-fed and cared for until it could survive independently in its natural environment.
