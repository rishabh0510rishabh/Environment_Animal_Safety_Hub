# Decline of Scavenger Species and Disease Risk - Feature Documentation

## Issue #1543: Decline of Scavenger Species and Disease Risk

### Overview
This educational feature examines how the decline of scavenger species, particularly vultures, creates cascading ecological consequences including increased carcass persistence, pathogen proliferation, and elevated disease transmission risks to wildlife, livestock, and humans. The page provides comprehensive information about scavenger ecology, population declines, and the public health implications of disrupted carcass removal services.

### Features Implemented

#### 1. **Educational Content Structure**
- Hero section introducing scavenger decline crisis
- Overview of scavenger ecological roles and population trends
- Detailed mechanisms of disease risk amplification
- Species-specific impact sections for vultures and other scavengers

#### 2. **Scavenger Ecology**
- Natural carcass removal services and ecosystem benefits
- Vulture population declines (99% in some species)
- Competition with other scavengers (crows, foxes, feral dogs)
- Cultural and ecological significance

#### 3. **Disease Risk Mechanisms**
- Carcass persistence and pathogen incubation
- Increased disease transmission to wildlife populations
- Livestock health impacts and economic consequences
- Zoonotic disease risks to human populations
- Rabies, anthrax, and other pathogen amplification

#### 4. **Case Studies**
- Asian vulture crisis and diclofenac poisoning
- African vulture declines and rabies spread
- European scavenger losses and disease patterns
- New World vulture population trends

#### 5. **Conservation Solutions**
- NSAID-free veterinary practices
- Poisoning prevention and enforcement
- Habitat protection and restoration
- Artificial feeding stations and supplementation
- Community education and engagement

#### 6. **Interactive Elements**
- Animated population decline charts
- Disease transmission pathway visualizations
- Scavenger species comparison tool
- Geographic impact mapping
